# Bank Reconciliation Simulation (Excel)

## Overview
This project demonstrates the process of performing a **bank reconciliation** using Microsoft Excel. The objective is to reconcile a company's cash book with the bank statement to ensure the accuracy of recorded cash balances.

The project simulates a real-world accounting task by identifying reconciling items and adjusting balances so that the book balance and bank balance match.

---

## Business Scenario
This exercise simulates the monthly bank reconciliation process for **Luna’s Local Goods**, a small business maintaining its own cash ledger.

At the end of the month, the company compares its internal cash records with the bank statement to identify discrepancies and ensure the accuracy of financial records.

## Tools Used
- Microsoft Excel
- Structured financial tables
- Basic Excel formulas

## Skills Demonstrated
- Bank reconciliation procedures
- Cash account verification
- Financial record comparison
- Identification of reconciling items
- Excel-based financial analysis


## Objective
The objective of this project is to demonstrate the ability to:

- Compare internal accounting records with bank records
- Identify reconciling items
- Adjust balances to determine the true cash balance
- Understand the bank reconciliation process used in accounting

Differences between the bank statement and company records may occur due to:
- Outstanding checks
- Deposits in transit
- Bank service charges
- Timing differences in transaction recording

## Reconciliation Process

To complete this reconciliation, I followed these steps:

1. Reviewed the ending balance in the company’s cash book and the ending balance on the bank statement.
2. Compared transactions recorded in the cash book against transactions shown on the bank statement.
3. Identified reconciling items, including outstanding checks, deposits in transit, and bank charges not yet recorded in the books.
4. Adjusted the bank balance for timing differences.
5. Adjusted the book balance for items appearing on the bank statement but not yet recorded internally.
6. Confirmed that the adjusted bank balance matched the adjusted book balance.
